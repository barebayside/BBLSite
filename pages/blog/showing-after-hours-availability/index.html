Showing your hours & after-hours options — so you win the responsiveness test

3 June 2026 6 min read

Someone searches for your service at 7pm on a Saturday. They find two listings: one with clear hours and a note saying "urgent bookings available outside standard hours," one with nothing. Both businesses do equally good work. One gets the call. Leaving hours blank doesn't look flexible — it looks like nobody's checked the listing in months.

1Hours are now a top-5 ranking factor — most businesses don't know that

Filling in your Google Business Profile hours isn't just a conversion fix. It's one of the five most important factors for ranking in Google's local map pack.

Whitespark's 2026 Local Search Ranking Factors report tracks signals across hundreds of local businesses. Business hours — specifically whether a profile has hours listed and whether the business is open at the time of search — moved into the top five local pack factors in their latest survey.

Business hours open at time of search = #5 most important local pack factor. — Whitespark, Nov 2025

The mechanism is simple: Google matches queries like "electrician open now" against your profile. If your hours confirm you're open, you're eligible. If they're blank, Google can't confirm availability — and a confirmed profile beats an ambiguous one.

Sterling Sky's research adds one nuance: profiles without hours can sometimes surface in after-hours searches, because Google treats absence as potential availability. But that's marginal and unreliable, and it costs you all your daytime ranking advantage. The right call is accurate hours.

2What "open now" filtering does to your visibility

When someone searches on mobile, "open now" is one of the most common filters they apply. If your hours aren't filled in, you don't appear — invisible at the exact moment someone is ready to call.

Voice search works identically. "Is [service type] near me open now?" pulls directly from GBP hours data. Wrong or missing hours means the answer is wrong or absent.

This is one of the highest-return fixes available: fifteen minutes, no cost, direct impact on ranking and visibility.

3How to set your standard hours correctly

In your Google Business Profile, fill in every day of the week:

  • Set "closed" for days you don't work — don't leave rows blank
  • Match your actual hours, not aspirational ones (listing 5:30pm when you stop at 4:30pm creates missed calls and friction)
  • Cross-check: your listing hours, website contact page, and any ad call extensions must match exactly

A mismatch across platforms chips away at trust before someone calls.

4Listing after-hours and emergency availability

If you take calls outside standard hours — even occasionally, even just for urgent jobs — put it on your listing. This is what most businesses skip.

Use your GBP business description field. One line is enough: "Emergency bookings available outside standard hours — call the main number." That single line does real work when someone is deciding whether to ring you or the next result.

If you offer a genuine on-call service, surface it clearly: description, website homepage, and contact page.

Don't over-promise: if you're reachable for emergencies some of the time, say "after-hours enquiries welcome" rather than "24/7 emergency service." The goal is accurate signal, not just winning the click.

5After-hours contact options — and what changed in 2024

Google's native in-profile chat was discontinued on 31 July 2024. Any guide recommending it is out of date.

What replaced it: Google now lets businesses connect a WhatsApp number to their profile (rolled out early 2025). Beyond that, practical after-hours contact options are phone, SMS, and a clear website enquiry form — whichever ones you'll actually respond to.

Tell searchers in your description: "Call after hours or leave a message and we'll get back to you by 8am" does more work than silence.

6Setting special hours for public holidays

Google has a dedicated "special hours" field. Use it before every public holiday — two minutes of work, done a week in advance.

Businesses that show as open when competitors are dark get the calls that would otherwise go unanswered. Set a recurring calendar reminder a week before each major holiday.

7Common mistakes to avoid

  • Leaving hours blank. Blank reads as unattended, not flexible.
  • Setting hours once and forgetting them. Any time your hours change, update the listing the same day.
  • Not mentioning after-hours availability. If you do it, say so. Silence gives the edge to a competitor who does.
  • Mismatching hours across platforms. Listing, website, and ads must match.
  • Skipping special hours before public holidays. The most avoidable bad-review trigger on this list.

Key takeaways

  • Top-5 ranking factor — Whitespark 2026 confirmed business hours open at time of search is now #5 in the local map pack.
  • "Open now" filtering is real — missing hours = invisible to anyone using that filter.
  • Blank fields lose callers — looks unattended, not flexible.
  • One line about after-hours separates you from competitors who stay silent.
  • Consistency across listing, website, and ads — any mismatch erodes trust before the call.
  • Special hours before every public holiday — two minutes prevents the bad review.
  • Google's native chat is gone (July 2024) — use phone, SMS, WhatsApp, or a website form.

Common questions

Can I show emergency callouts without being available 24/7?

Yes. Use "after-hours enquiries welcome" or "emergency bookings available — call the main number." You don't need round-the-clock availability to signal more responsiveness than a business that says nothing.

What if my hours genuinely vary week to week?

Set the hours you reliably keep and note in your description that hours can vary. Under-promise, be reachable — that's better than listing hours you can't honour.

How often should I check my GBP hours?

Every time your hours change, and before every public holiday. A quarterly habit check takes two minutes.

Does correct hours data genuinely help ranking?

Yes. Whitespark's 2026 report puts business hours open at time of search at #5 for local map pack. It's one of the few ranking improvements achievable in under fifteen minutes.

My website shows different hours to my Google listing. Which takes priority?

Neither — the conflict is the problem. Fix both to match and pick your website as the single source of truth going forward.

Can I still use Google's in-profile chat?

No. Google shut it down on 31 July 2024. Connect WhatsApp to your profile, or direct after-hours enquiries to phone, SMS, or your website contact form.
