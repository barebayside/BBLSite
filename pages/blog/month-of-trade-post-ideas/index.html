A month of local-trade post ideas

3 June 2026 7 min read

Monday rolls around and you're staring at your phone — then nothing, and next week is the same story. That's a blank-page problem, not a motivation problem. This guide fixes it: four weeks of ready prompts, a system for turning one job into multiple posts, and the platform rules that apply in 2026.

1The four post types that work

Not every post needs to sell. The ones that sell hardest usually don't look like they're selling.

  • Teach — a tip, a common mistake, how something works. Builds trust before they've called you.
  • Show — the work in progress, before-and-after, the van loaded at 6am. Real, unpolished, local.
  • Prove — a review, a happy message, a result. Social proof from real customers beats any ad copy.
  • Ask — a question, a poll, an invitation to reply. DM shares and saves now drive more reach than likes (Sprout Social, Instagram Algorithm 2026).

Ratio that works: three or four teach/show/prove posts for every one promotional post. Give value first.

2You don't need a big following

Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok all run on an interest graph — content reaches people based on interest, not just who follows you (Digital Applied, 2026). Over half of Instagram Reels views come from non-followers (Loopex Digital). Make content worth watching; don't chase the follower count.

3Short video and consistency

Reels, Facebook short video, TikTok, YouTube Shorts — all get far more distribution than static posts. Phone + window light + a $30–50 clip-on lapel mic gets you 90% there. Shoot vertical. Add captions — most people watch muted. A 15–30 second before-and-after clip outperforms a static photo of the same job.

Three solid posts per week beats a burst followed by silence. Buffer's analysis of 100,000+ accounts found consistent posting delivers roughly five times more engagement than sporadic bursts. Local recognition compounds: people keep seeing your name and face — when they need what you offer, familiar wins.

4Four weeks of ready prompts

Add your own voice and local details. That's what makes them work.

CTA rule: Never drop a link in a Facebook or Instagram caption. Facebook suppresses link posts for non-verified pages; Instagram doesn't allow clickable links in captions (Social Media Examiner, 2026). Use: "link in bio," Stories with swipe-up, link in first comment, or ask people to DM you.

Week 1 — Build trust

  • Mon (Teach): "One thing most people don't check before calling a [plumber / cleaner / arborist]…" One useful tip, kept short. End: "Save this if it's handy."
  • Wed (Show): Before-and-after from a recent job. Two photos or a Reel. Caption: the problem, what you did, which area. CTA: "DM us if you've got the same thing going on."
  • Fri (Prove): Recent review or happy customer message (with permission). Screenshot or quote with first name. CTA: "Really appreciate it, [Name] — means a lot."

Week 2 — Give value, invite conversation

  • Mon (Teach): "Here's what causes [blocked drains / peeling paint / dead lawn patches] — and when it's worth calling someone." Educate, don't advertise. End: "Share this with someone who needs it."
  • Wed (Show): Behind-the-scenes. Tools laid out before a big job, team loading up at 6am. CTA: "This is how the day starts — what does yours look like?"
  • Fri (Ask): "Quick question — when did you last [service your hot water system / clear your gutters]?" A poll or open question. Gets replies and saves, which the algorithm rewards.

Week 3 — Build local recognition

  • Mon (Teach): Seasonal tip — storm prep, summer heat, pre-winter checks. Something timely that shows you know your area. CTA: "Save this for when the weather turns."
  • Wed (Show): A job you're proud of. Walk through what made it tricky and how you fixed it. Short Reel works especially well here.
  • Fri (Prove): A review that mentions your area. Caption: "Another happy customer in [area] — thanks [Name]!" Hyperlocal proof travels well in community Facebook Groups (Pilot Poster, 2026).

Week 4 — Soft sell and reset

  • Mon (Teach): "Signs you need a [service] sooner than you think." Checklist format — easy to read, save, and share.
  • Wed (Show): Transformation post. Before / during / after. Tell the story: what they called about, what you found, what it looks like now.
  • Fri (Ask/Offer): Soft CTA — not "buy now." Try: "We've had a few spots come up for [month] — worth a DM if you've been putting off [service]." Or: "Full details in our bio."

5Facebook Groups outperform Pages

Facebook Page posts reach 1–6% of followers. Facebook Groups reach 20–40% of members per post (Pilot Poster, 2026). Don't spam groups — most have rules against it. Join your local community group, answer questions in your trade area, share something useful occasionally. A helpful reply to "can anyone recommend a good [trade]?" is worth more than ten promotional page posts.

6Turn one job into four posts

  • The problem post — photo of what the customer called about, one sentence explaining it.
  • The process post — 15-second vertical clip mid-job. Add captions so it works muted.
  • The result post — finished job, two photos side by side or a carousel. Caption: what changed and which area.
  • The review post — when the customer leaves a review, screenshot it and post a brief thank-you.

Four posts from one job. Three jobs a week gives you twelve pieces of content. You'll never start from a blank page.

7Getting people to your website

  • Keep your bio link updated; say "full details in our bio."
  • On Facebook, drop the link in the first comment — avoids the algorithm penalty for links in post bodies.
  • Instagram Stories with swipe-up if available.
  • Include your area in captions, not just hashtags. "Heading to [neighbourhood] for a fence install" is picked up by location algorithms. Location hashtags are largely ignored now, especially on Facebook.

8Common mistakes to avoid

  • Posting links in caption bodies on Facebook or Instagram
  • Posting in bursts then disappearing — kills the local recognition you're building
  • Only posting finished results — in-progress, imperfect content often performs better
  • Writing every caption like an ad — conversational beats promotional
  • Ignoring comments and DMs — a short reply turns a post into a conversation, which platforms reward
  • Stressing about follower count — the interest graph surfaces good content to non-followers regardless

Key takeaways

  • Teach / show / prove / ask mix — give value most of the time; sell occasionally.
  • One job = four posts — problem, process, result, review. Never start blank.
  • Consistency beats volume — three solid posts a week held over months (Buffer).
  • Short vertical video is the reach lever — add captions, shoot muted-friendly.
  • Interest graph, not follow graph — good content reaches non-followers (Digital Applied).
  • No links in post bodies — use bio, Stories, or first comment.
  • Facebook Groups beat Pages for organic reach — be genuinely helpful, not promotional.
  • Saves and DM shares beat likes — aim for posts people bookmark or send to a friend.

Common questions

How far ahead should I plan?

A week at a time. Pick a day — Sunday evening or Monday morning — and line up the week.

Do I need professional photos?

No. Phone and decent light. Authenticity matters more than camera quality; over-produced content often underperforms.

Which platform should I focus on?

Where your customers already are. For most local service businesses that's Facebook, followed by Instagram. Master one before adding a second. A Reel posted to both at once is a low-effort way to test video reach.

How do I know if it's working?

Profile views, website clicks, message enquiries — all free in platform insights. Better still: ask new customers how they heard about you. "Saw your posts" shows up more than you'd expect.

What if I run out of ideas?

Post about a job from earlier in the month you haven't covered yet. Or answer a question you get regularly — "we get asked this a lot, here's the answer" writes itself.
