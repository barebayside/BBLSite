The mentions that make AI trust a local business

3 June 2026 5 min read

When someone asks an AI assistant to recommend a local business, it doesn't pull an answer from thin air. It looks for evidence — mentions, listings, and references across the web that corroborate what your own website says. If that evidence is thin or missing, your business gets skipped.

1What AI actually uses when it recommends you

Roughly 60% of citations in AI-generated local results go to third-party publishers — review sites, directories, editorial sites — not to the business's own website. [Whitespark, May 2025]

AI assistants — ChatGPT, Google's AI Overviews, Perplexity — cross-reference your site against what independent sources say. Think of it as a reference check: your CV says you're great; the AI rings around to confirm it. Citations do more than list your details — AI reads the context around them (reviews, descriptions, editorial framing) to determine what you do, where you operate, and whether you're credible.

2ChatGPT and Google use different sources — both matter

Most businesses know to be on Google. Far fewer know that ChatGPT doesn't use Google Business Profile at all.

ChatGPT pulls local business data from Bing Places for Business and Bing's web index, which includes Yelp, TripAdvisor, Facebook Pages, and directory listings. [Local Falcon, November 2025] Google's AI Overviews use Google's own data — so optimising your Google Business Profile helps there, but does nothing for ChatGPT or Microsoft Copilot.

In late 2025, Microsoft overhauled Bing Places and merged it into bing.com/forbusiness. [Streetlight Local, 2025] Most businesses have never claimed their listing — it's either missing or auto-generated from old data.

Yelp matters separately: OpenAI licensed Yelp's business data in 2025, so Yelp reviews and profiles now directly feed ChatGPT responses. [Yelp IR, October 2025]

The split:
Google Business Profile → Google AI Overviews, AI Mode
Bing Places + Yelp → ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot

3Why inconsistent information quietly kills your chances

Fix this before adding new listings: AI treats mismatched business details as uncertainty signals, not minor formatting differences. "Smith Plumbing" on your website, "Smith's Plumbing Co." on Google, and "Smith Plumbing and Gas" on a directory — that's three different businesses to an AI.

What must match exactly across every platform:

  • Business name
  • Address (including unit number, floor, postcode)
  • Phone number with country code
  • Website URL
  • Business category

Copy from one master record — never retype. Consistency is a stronger trust signal than fifty half-accurate listings.

4The directories and mentions worth your time

Not all citations carry the same weight. A listing that exists purely to sell listings — thin content, no real visitors — does almost nothing. Quality matters far more than volume.

Essential platforms

  • Google Business Profile — starting point for Google's AI. Complete it: accurate category, description, photos, service areas, trading hours, website link.
  • Bing Places for Business — the one most businesses haven't claimed. Direct lever on ChatGPT and Copilot visibility. Microsoft's 2025 overhaul made it straightforward to manage.
  • Yelp — feeds ChatGPT directly via the OpenAI data licence. Genuine reviews signal trust to multiple AI platforms simultaneously.
  • Apple Maps — large share of mobile searches, especially iPhone users. With the Apple + Google Gemini deal announced January 2026, Apple's AI features increasingly draw on Google's ecosystem. [CNBC, January 2026]

Worth prioritising

  • Industry and trade directories — association sites, licensing body registers, trade federation directories carry more authority than dozens of generic listings.
  • Local business directories — Chamber of Commerce sites, local government directories. These corroborate your geographic context.

The quick test: does it have real visitors and editorial standards, or does it exist to charge businesses for listings nobody reads?

5How to earn mentions AI actually trusts

The most credible citations aren't ones you buy — they're ones other organisations create because there's a genuine reason to name you.

  • Local media and community sites — stories in local outlets, neighbourhood website mentions, community page write-ups — are exactly the independent, non-commercial references AI treats as strong authority signals. [Whitespark, 2026 Local Search Ranking Factors] Consider whether you've done anything newsworthy: a local event, an unusual project, a community service. A pitch to a local journalist costs nothing but time.
  • Supplier and partner mentions — if you're an authorised dealer or installer, many manufacturers maintain a "find a local pro" page. Getting listed puts your name on a well-credentialled domain AI already trusts.
  • Professional association membership — a listing on an authoritative domain plus a signal that a third party has vetted you.
  • Genuine guest contributions — a practical article for a local trade site, a tips piece in a community newsletter — earn byline mentions naturally. The contribution must be genuinely useful, not a thinly disguised advert.

6Getting listed accurately where it counts

Build a master record first: one document with your exact business name, address (with unit/postcode), phone with country code, website URL, and a short description. Every listing gets copied from it — never retyped.

Claim existing listings before creating new ones. Most major directories already have a stub for your business, auto-generated from public data. Correcting it is better than leaving an inaccurate stub live and creating a duplicate.

Write a specific business description for each profile. A clear description of what you do, who you serve, and where you operate gives AI the context it needs to match you to the right queries. Specific and accurate beats long and vague.

7Tracking where you're already named

Search your business name in quotes alongside your area and service. For each result, verify name, address, phone, and website against your master record. Also search your phone number and address in quotes — older businesses often have ghost listings with outdated details on platforms they never signed up to.

Free SEO tools offer a basic citation audit that flags mismatches. Running one every six to twelve months is a sensible habit.

8Common mistakes to avoid

  • Quantity over quality. Twenty listings on trusted platforms outperform two hundred on obscure directories.
  • GBP-only focus. ChatGPT uses Bing Places and Yelp, not GBP. Businesses that skip Bing are invisible to a large share of AI users.
  • Claiming and forgetting. Review every listing every six months — hours, location, and services change.
  • Adding citations on top of inconsistency. Fix what's already out there first. More inaccurate listings compounds the problem.
  • Keyword-stuffed descriptions. Plain, specific copy outperforms a list of search terms.

Key takeaways

  • ~60% of AI citations go to third-party sources, not your own website. [Whitespark, May 2025]
  • ChatGPT uses Bing Places and Yelp, not Google Business Profile — claim and complete your Bing listing; most businesses haven't.
  • Consistency is foundational. Name, address, phone, and website must match exactly across every listing before you add more.
  • Quality beats quantity. Industry associations, local media, and trade directories carry more authority than generic aggregators.
  • Earned mentions beat paid listings. Local press, supplier directories, and association membership produce citations AI treats as independent validation.
  • Audit before you add. Find where you're already listed, fix what's wrong, then expand.

Common questions

Do I need to be on every directory I can find?

No. A handful of accurate, credible listings beats dozens of thin or inconsistent ones. Focus on platforms with real traffic, editorial standards, or industry relevance.

Does it matter if a listing doesn't link back to my site?

An unlinked mention — your business name, address, and phone on a credible site — still signals to AI that you're a real, established business. Always fill the website field if it exists.

How long does it take for new citations to make a difference?

Most businesses see gradual improvement over two to four months. Steady and accurate over time outperforms a rushed burst of new listings.

What if I find a listing I never created and can't claim?

Search the platform's help section for "report a listing" or "request removal." One prominent listing with the wrong phone number costs real enquiries.

Is there a difference between citations for AI search versus regular local SEO?

The foundation is the same — consistent, accurate mentions on trusted sources. A strong local SEO citation profile also builds AI credibility. The goals aren't in conflict.
