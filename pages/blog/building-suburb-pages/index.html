Building suburb pages that rank — examples, pros & cons

3 June 2026 6 min read

You've read that suburb pages help local businesses rank on Google. Now you're staring at thirty suburbs you technically service and wondering which ones to build, how many, and whether building a lot of them will quietly make things worse. This guide answers all of that.

1Why this is a strategy question, not a writing question

The companion guide Writing a Suburb Page That Ranks (and Doesn't Sound Fake) covers how to write one page well. This guide covers which suburbs, how many, in what order, and how they connect.

Get the strategy wrong and the writing doesn't matter. A hundred well-written pages that all look the same to Google get treated as junk.

2What suburb pages can and can't do

Two sections of Google's local results. Only one responds to suburb pages.

  • The map box (three listings with pins) is driven primarily by proximity — how close your business is to the searcher (Sterling Sky, 2025). A suburb page won't shift it.
  • The website results below the map box are driven by relevance. A well-written page about real work you do in a specific suburb can rank there for "[your service] [suburb]" searches. That's the realistic prize.

3How many suburb pages to build

The target range for most local service businesses: 6–15 pages.

  • 6–10 if you serve a compact area
  • Up to 15 for a wider metro
  • Beyond 15, quality almost always drops

Industry research from Sterling Sky and BrightLocal puts "a few dozen" as the workable ceiling. Every suburb page has to be genuinely different — which takes real effort. Trying to produce fifty pages quickly forces identical structure, and that's what triggers a penalty.

A handful of genuinely ranking pages is worth more than a large set that collects no traffic.

4The 80/20 content rule

About 80% of each page can be shared content — your service description, contact details, core offer. About 20% must be completely unique to that suburb. That 20% does all the work.

Sterling Sky reviewed 35 suburb pages with 84% shared content across them. They still ranked and generated leads — because the shared content was genuinely useful and the local detail was real.

The question Google and real readers ask is the same: does this page exist to help someone, or only to rank?

5Which suburbs to pick

Two things must both be true:

  1. Real search demand for your service in that suburb
  2. You actually work there — not just in theory

Check demand first

  • Type "[your service] [suburb name]" into Google and check autocomplete. If Google suggests it, people are searching for it.
  • Check Google Search Console filtered by suburb name to find impressions you're not capturing.
  • A local trade can justify a page for 50 searches a month.

Then check fit

Go through your job records — invoices, job sheets, messages. Which suburbs appear repeatedly? Start there. You have the local knowledge and likely have jobs to reference.

Avoid pages for suburbs you've visited twice or wish to break into. You can't write a genuine page for a place you don't know.

6Page structure

Use a consistent structure, but fill each page with suburb-specific content so no two read the same.

  • Opening: What makes jobs in that suburb distinct — property age, typical requests, access challenges. Lead with the work, not the suburb name.
  • Your service there: What you do, why people call you. One or two paragraphs. Mention the suburb naturally, not in every sentence.
  • Real job or local detail: Describe actual work without inventing details. If you haven't worked there, write about common job types based on the area's building stock.
  • One suburb-specific question answered: Heritage rules in an older suburb? Stormwater in a new estate? One paragraph answering something real gives the page a reason to exist.
  • Contact and next step: Phone number on the page. Clear sentence on your offer.

7Good vs bad examples

Bad (doorway page)

"Are you looking for the best cleaning service in [your suburb]? Our [your suburb] cleaning team is ready to help. We offer professional cleaning in [your suburb] and surrounding areas. Call our [your suburb] cleaners today."

Swap the suburb name for any other and nothing changes. Google knows. It earns nothing — and can drag down pages that were already ranking.

Good (genuine page)

"Most of the work we pick up in [your suburb] is from renters moving out of the longer-term share houses on the east side — they need thorough end-of-lease cleans that hold up to landlord inspection. We also get owner-occupiers in the older terraces wanting quarterly deep cleans, especially kitchens with original cabinetry."

A local reads that and nods. It's different from the page for a neighbouring suburb because the jobs there genuinely are different. The gap between these two isn't effort — it's whether you used the knowledge you already have.

8Internal links

  • Each suburb page → your main services page. Takes suburb-search visitors to the page that converts them into enquiries.
  • Your main services page → each suburb page. Hub and spokes: main page links out; each suburb page links back. Google uses this to understand the set is connected.
  • Adjacent suburbs → each other, where natural. "We also service [nearby suburb]" is legitimate and helps both pages.
  • No orphan pages. A suburb page not linked from anywhere on your site is harder for Google to find and reads as filler.

9The doorway page risk

Google's spam policy explicitly names doorway pages, including "multiple domain names or pages targeted at specific regions or cities" (Google Search Central). The risk isn't just that those pages don't rank — a set of doorway pages can damage your entire site's trust and pull down rankings you already have.

Google also targets scaled content abuse: pages generated to manipulate rankings rather than help users — including AI-generated location pages where only the suburb name changes. Enforcement tightened through 2025 and is ongoing.

The test for every page: Would a real person from that suburb find this useful? If yes, build it. If the honest answer is no, don't.

10In what order to build

  1. Start with the suburb outside your home base where you do the most work.
  2. Publish it. Let it settle a few weeks.
  3. Move to the next most familiar suburb — based on job history, not wishful thinking.
  4. Build one or two pages at a time. A slow rollout looks natural; a large batch in one week looks like a content farm.
  5. Before each new page goes live, add it to your main services page's internal links.

Key takeaways

  • Suburb pages win the website results, not the map box. Proximity drives the map box — suburb pages target the organic results below it.
  • 6–15 pages is the realistic target. Hundreds of near-identical pages put you in doorway-page and scaled-content-abuse territory (both named in Google's spam policy).
  • 80% solid service content, 20% genuinely suburb-specific. That 20% earns the ranking.
  • Pick suburbs by demand AND fit. Job records and invoices are your best guide.
  • Hub-and-spoke internal links. Main page links out to suburb pages; each suburb page links back. No orphans.
  • Doorway pages hurt the whole site — not just the pages themselves.
  • Build slowly. Two pages over a few weeks looks genuine. Twenty in a day looks like a content run.

Common questions

Should I target every suburb in my metro area?

No. Only suburbs where you genuinely work and can write from experience. For most businesses that's 6–15 pages.

Two suburbs I service are very similar — same jobs, same property types. Separate pages?

Write them, but focus on what's different — street character, access challenges, customer types. If you genuinely can't find differences, one page covering both may be better.

Will building suburb pages affect my existing rankings?

Done properly — genuine pages, gradual rollout, good internal links — they complement existing rankings. Done badly, they can harm them.

My competitor has a page for every area. Should I match them?

Check whether those pages are actually ranking, not just whether they exist. Many suburb page sets collect no traffic.

Do I update suburb pages after publishing?

Yes. Add real detail when you do notable work there. Pages updated with genuine content over time outperform static ones.
