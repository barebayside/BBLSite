Building an answers page AI will quote

3 June 2026 7 min read

People type questions into ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google's AI Overview — "who's a reliable builder near me," "how much does this usually cost" — and the AI names specific businesses. Most of the time it's not the best business around. It's the one with the clearest, most useful text in the right places.

1Google's AI and ChatGPT don't search the same web

This matters before you do anything else.

  • Google AI Overviews and AI Mode pull from Google's own index: your Google Business Profile, your website, Google reviews.
  • ChatGPT searches Bing, not Google (Search Engine Land confirmed this). It uses Bing Places for Business, Yelp, TripAdvisor, Facebook Pages, and the wider Bing index.

A business that only focuses on Google is invisible to ChatGPT. Cover both ecosystems — they don't overlap as much as most people assume.

2Which questions actually trigger AI answers

Not every search produces an AI response. Whitespark's study of 540 local queries found:

  • Plain "near me" searches → AI Overviews appear ~15% of the time
  • Hybrid questions ("how much does [service] cost in [city]") → AI Overviews appear 97% of the time

The questions your customers ask before they call are exactly the ones that trigger AI answers most reliably:

  • "How long does this job usually take?"
  • "Do I need a permit for this in [state/province]?"
  • "What's included in a standard quote?"
  • "How do I know if I need the bigger version?"

Write twenty down. Pick the ten to fifteen that come up most often. Each one becomes a section on your answers page.

3How to write answers AI tools will actually quote

AI tools don't lift whole pages. They pull short, self-contained passages that answer a clear question. Format matters as much as content.

  • Question as heading. Not "About Our Services." Write "How long does [the job] usually take?" — the exact words a person would type or say.
  • Answer first. First sentence delivers the answer. "In most cases, a standard [service] takes two to three hours." Supporting detail comes after. No preamble.
  • Plain language. Write the way you'd explain it on the phone. Skip trade jargon or immediately define it.
  • Two to four sentences per answer. If a question genuinely needs more, split it into two questions. Shorter, denser answers are extracted more reliably.
  • Name where you operate. "Serving the greater [city] area" or "for [region] residents" tells the AI exactly where your business is relevant. Generic answers lose to specific ones.

The local sites we audit almost never have an answers page at all. When they do, it's buried, hard to read, and written in corporate language nobody searches for. The bar is genuinely low.

4Your website is only part of the picture

About 60% of AI citations go to third-party publishers — directories, review platforms, local media, Reddit, editorial sites — not to the business's own website (Whitespark, May 2025, 540-query study). Your answers page is roughly half the job.

When Yelp, a local industry directory, a supplier's partner page, and your own site all say consistent things about your business, AI tools treat that as a reliable signal. One source alone is weaker than three or four saying the same thing.

The sibling guide — The Mentions That Make AI Trust a Local Business — covers off-site presence. Neither strategy works as well without the other.

5Setting up the page

You don't need a developer.

  1. Create a new page called "Questions & Answers" or "Common Questions" — a real page with its own URL, not a collapsed accordion buried on your homepage.
  2. Use question-style headings (H2 or H3 in most website builders). Write the answer directly beneath each one.
  3. Link to the page from your navigation or footer. A page with no links pointing to it is much harder for search engines and AI tools to find.
  4. Update it every few months. Pages updated within the last three to six months are cited more frequently than stale ones (Whitespark). It's a twenty-minute job, not a project.

6Reviews and listings still matter

  • Review volume and recency are trust signals — especially for Google AI Mode, which surfaces reviews as primary data. Businesses recommended by ChatGPT average 4.3 stars across platforms (SOCi 2026 Local Visibility Index).
  • Consistent NAP (name, address, phone number) across your website, Google Business Profile, and every directory you're listed on. Inconsistencies cause AI systems to second-guess the data.
  • Claim your Bing Places listing if you haven't. That's the direct feed into ChatGPT's local results.

7What to avoid

  • Keyword stuffing. Packing phrases in unnaturally makes answers harder to read and less likely to be quoted.
  • Hiding it behind a form. A page locked behind a contact wall is invisible to AI tools. Keep it open.
  • Long answers. Six-to-eight sentence answers are harder to extract than two-to-four sentence ones.
  • Unsourced multipliers. "FAQ pages are X times more likely to appear in AI answers" circulates widely, but the methodology behind most versions is undisclosed. The honest version: structured content and FAQ format help AI tools read your page more reliably — that's supported. Exact multipliers aren't.

Key takeaways

  • Google's AI and ChatGPT use different data sources. GBP and Google serve one; Bing Places and third-party directories serve the other.
  • Hybrid questions trigger AI answers far more than "near me" searches. Write answers for those.
  • About 60% of AI citations go to third-party sites. Off-site mentions matter as much as your own page (Whitespark, May 2025).
  • Format counts. Question as heading, short direct answer underneath — that's the structure AI tools extract.
  • Pages updated within three to six months are cited more often than stale ones (Whitespark).

Common questions

How many questions should I start with?

Ten to fifteen is enough to launch. Cover the ones that come up most in calls and enquiries. A published page beats a perfect one still being written.

Does the page need special code or schema markup?

Standard headings and paragraphs are fine. FAQ schema can help with machine readability, but it's not a guarantee of AI citations — there's no shortcut that bypasses quality filters.

Will this help with regular Google search too?

Yes. A well-structured, locally specific answers page tends to perform in both traditional search results and AI-generated answers.

How quickly will this make a difference?

It depends on your reviews, directory presence, and how competitive your area is. A business with solid reviews and a few web mentions can start appearing in AI answers within weeks. Starting from scratch takes longer.

What about ChatGPT specifically?

Claim and optimise your Bing Places listing, make sure you're on Yelp and major directories with consistent details, and build editorial mentions. Your website content matters too, but only if Bing has indexed it.
