Your first Google Search campaign — step by step

3 June 2026 6 min read

Most local service businesses that tried Google Ads have a story about burning through budget and getting nothing back. The problem is almost never Google Ads — it's the setup. This guide sets it up correctly: a Search campaign aimed at people ready to book, pointed at the right page, with guardrails so budget doesn't leak.

1Start with Search, not the "smart" options

Google pushes new advertisers toward Performance Max and "Smart" campaigns. Ignore them for now.

  • Performance Max runs across Search, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, and Display simultaneously. It needs 30+ conversions a month to learn — a new campaign has none.
  • A standard Search campaign shows text ads only when someone types a matching search. You control targeting, see exactly what triggered your ads, and understand what's working before handing the wheel to an AI.
  • AI Max (Google's 2026 upgrade that auto-expands keyword matching and ad assets) and PMax are worth exploring once Search is profitable. Not before.

2Should you also look at Local Services Ads?

Local Services Ads (LSAs) sit above all other search results. They show your name, star rating, and a "Google Verified" badge (Google unified its old "Guaranteed" and "Screened" badges into one blue badge in October 2025). You pay per lead, not per click — and no keyword management required.

If your category is eligible (plumbing, electrical, cleaning, HVAC, legal, financial services, and 70-plus others in 2026), set up LSAs alongside your Search campaign.

Watch-out: Google tightened its LSA credit policy in 2025 — no longer credits leads for job types you don't offer or areas outside your service zone. Set category and service-area settings precisely. Per-lead costs vary by service, city, and country; treat figures you see online as rough indicators.

3How keywords work in 2026

Three match types control which searches trigger your ads:

  • Exact match — no longer strict. In 2026 it includes close variants: misspellings, plurals, reordered words, same-intent paraphrases. [emergency plumber] may match "urgent plumbing service."
  • Phrase match — the meaning of your phrase must be present; other words can appear around it.
  • Broad match — Google's AI decides relevance using your landing page, keywords, and searcher context. Too loose without conversion history.

Start with phrase match. Move to broad only after you have real conversion data.

Negative keywords are now your main control lever

Because match types have loosened, ads can appear for searches you never intended. Google also hides a large proportion of triggering search terms ("low volume"), so you can't always see where spend went.

A negative keyword tells Google to exclude any search containing that term. Add your list before you go live under Keywords → Negative Keywords:

  • DIY, how to, tutorial, YouTube, video
  • jobs, career, apprenticeship, training, course, certificate
  • what is, do I need, definition, meaning
  • free, cheap (if you're not competing on price)
  • reviews

Fifteen minutes now prevents consistent leakage. One caution: don't over-negate once you switch to Smart Bidding — too many exclusions starves the algorithm.

4Pick your starting keywords

High-intent local keywords follow a simple pattern: service + location, often with an urgency signal:

  • "emergency plumber [suburb]"
  • "same-day cleaner [suburb]"
  • "roof repair [city]"
  • "air conditioning service [area] available now"

Start with ten to twenty phrases covering your most profitable service and two or three areas. Spreading too thin is how budgets vanish without usable data. Google increasingly marks very granular keywords "Inactive" — broaden the phrase or remove it.

5Match every ad to its landing page

If someone searches "landscaper [your suburb]" and clicks your ad, they should land on a page about landscaping in [your suburb] — not your homepage. Two things depend on this:

  • Quality Score — Google's 1–10 rating of keyword, ad, and landing page relevance. Ad Rank formula: bid × Quality Score + expected impact of ad assets. A lower bid with a strong Quality Score can outrank a higher bid with a weak one. Landing page experience is consistently the most underinvested component.
  • Conversion rate — a person who lands on a matching page is far more likely to call than someone navigating from a homepage.

No suburb-specific pages yet? A service page that clearly mentions the area is better than nothing. Each matching page also improves organic rankings.

6Set a tight location radius

Target a radius around your base (20–30 km is a common starting point) or select specific suburbs and postcodes. Change the location option to "Presence" only — ads show to people physically in your area, not someone elsewhere who once searched your suburb.

7Write ad copy that does one job

Up to three headlines (30 characters each) and two descriptions (90 characters each):

  • Headline 1: The service — "Same-Day Cleaning Service"
  • Headline 2: The location — "Serving [Your Area]"
  • Headline 3: Reason to call — "Fully Insured & Available Today"
  • Descriptions: Restate service and area; direct call to action — "Call for a free quote" or "Book online today."

Don't be clever. The person who clicked already decided they need help — confirm you're the right choice and make the next step obvious.

Add a call asset and a lead form asset (Google renamed "extensions" to "assets" in 2022). A call asset lets mobile users tap to call without clicking through; a lead form asset lets them submit details without leaving Google. Both free.

8Choose the right bidding strategy

For a new campaign, use Maximise Conversions. Set a daily budget cap; Google gets as many conversions as possible within it. No target cost per lead needed — you're collecting data.

Once you have 30+ conversions in 30 days, move to Target CPA (cost per acquisition). Set it at or slightly above your current average — too low limits the auctions you enter. Don't use manual bidding to start; without conversion data the AI has nothing to learn from.

9Set a starting budget with the right mindset

What is a single new job worth to you in margin? What portion would you spend to find out whether this channel works? That's your daily figure — you're buying information, not guaranteed jobs. All dollar figures here are hypothetical; costs per click vary by service, city, and competition.

10Wait before reading results

Give the campaign two to three weeks before drawing conclusions. Confirm ads are running (Status: Eligible or Active), clicks are coming in, and your phone and contact form work. Don't touch bids or keywords in the first two weeks.

After two to three weeks, review in order:

  • Calls and form submissions — the number that matters
  • Clicks — landing page traffic
  • Cost per click — context, not a goal
  • Search terms report — what triggered ads; informs the next round of negatives

Clicks but no calls = landing page problem. Very few clicks = bids too low or thin local search volume.

Key takeaways

  • Search campaign first — PMax and AI Max come after it's profitable.
  • Check for Local Services Ads in your category — above all other ads, Google Verified badge, pay per lead.
  • Exact match is no longer strict — negative keywords are your primary spend control. Add them before launch.
  • Phrase match to start — broad match only once you have conversion data.
  • Match every ad to its landing page — raises Quality Score, lowers cost per click.
  • "Presence only" location targeting — tight radius, no tourists.
  • Maximise Conversions bidding to start; switch to Target CPA at 30+ conversions in 30 days.
  • Wait two to three weeks — calls and enquiries are the number that counts.

Common questions

Do I need a big budget to get started?

No. Modest and consistent beats large and brief. Two to three weeks of steady data is more useful than a one-week blitz.

What if I don't have suburb-specific pages?

Use a service page that mentions the area. Build suburb pages as you go — each improves ad relevance and organic rankings.

How do I know if it's working?

Count calls and form submissions from the campaign. Set up conversion tracking before spending a dollar.

Should I run Google Ads and Meta ads at the same time?

Not for your first campaign. Search catches people already looking; Meta reaches people who haven't started. Get your cost per acquisition from Search first.

Is Local Services Ads the same as Google Ads?

Separate systems, separate billing. LSAs sit above regular ads, charge per lead, and require verification (background check, licence, insurance). You can run both at once.
