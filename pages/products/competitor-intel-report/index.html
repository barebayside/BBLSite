What you get in the report

Every rival is scored across the same 8 channels, so you're comparing apples to apples.

  • 1
    An 8-section scorecard per rival.

    Ads, reviews, SEO, social, pricing, website, service gaps, messaging — each scored out of 100 with the evidence attached. So you know exactly where each rival is strong, where they're weak, and where you can move first.

  • 2
    Every live ad they're running — hook, offer, creative, landing page.

    Meta + Google, pulled and categorised by what they're saying and what they're offering. So you can steal winners, skip losers, and never launch a campaign blind again.

  • 3
    The review complaint patterns nobody's fixing.

    Customers are already telling you what's wrong with your competitors — one star at a time. We mine thousands of reviews and show you the repeating themes. So every complaint becomes a door you can walk through.

  • 4
    Service gaps nobody in your category is filling.

    Demand cross-referenced against supply. Where people are buying things your rivals don't sell. So you find the opening before the first-mover closes it.

  • 5
    The "fear callout" — the one move that should worry you most.

    Out of everything we find, one thing matters more than the rest. We call it out explicitly, with the evidence and a response plan. So you know what to fix this Friday, not in six months.

  • 6
    A ranked 90-day plan — this week, this month, this quarter.

    Not 40 "ideas." Actions, prioritised by impact-vs-effort, with the order to run them in. So you leave the report with a calendar, not a headache.

  • 7
    Plain English. No jargon. No marketing speak.

    If your dad can't read it in 40 minutes, we haven't finished. So you don't need a translator to act on it.

  • 8
    A live online dashboard — with one-click PDF export.

    Log in and drill into any specific ad, review, page, or score; export a clean PDF from the dashboard whenever you want one to read offline or forward. So the whole team works off the same picture, not three different meeting summaries.

Questions people ask before they buy

Who are these reports best for?

Best fit (B2C): local service businesses (plumbers, salons, daycares, clinics, gyms, trades), e-commerce / DTC brands, online service providers selling to consumers, creators and personal brands with a public following, and small SaaS selling to small businesses.

Also works (B2B service-style): agencies, consultancies, professional services (law/accounting), and B2B SaaS — provided you have a meaningful public presence (website, content, LinkedIn, reviews, ads).

Not suitable: B2B industrial / wholesale / trade-only businesses (where sales happen via reps, RFPs and trade shows, not public channels), enterprise-sales-only businesses, and adult content / OnlyFans-style creators (public data on those platforms is too restricted for the quality of report we deliver). If that's you, please don't order — message us at service@barebaysidelabs.com first.
