Leads are coming in but you're losing most of them before you even pick up the phone. You're not alone — we audit trades businesses across Melbourne and the story's always the same: plenty of enquiries, zero follow-up system. The fix isn't working harder. It's building an automated follow-up sequence that runs without you, 24/7. Here's exactly how to set one up.

## Why Most Small Businesses Lose Leads

The problem isn't that you don't have enough leads. It's that the ones you have aren't being followed up.

> **80% of sales require at least 5 follow-up contacts, but 44% of salespeople give up after just one attempt.** On top of that, only 37% of businesses respond to a new lead within one hour (Harvard Business Review). At Bare Bayside Labs, we see this pattern in almost every trades business we audit — the leads are there, but nobody's chasing them.

Here's what actually happens. A potential customer fills out your contact form on a Tuesday arvo. You're on a job site. By the time you check your emails Wednesday morning, they've already called two other businesses and booked one of them.

That's not a marketing problem. That's a follow-up problem.

## What an Automated Follow-Up System Actually Looks Like

It's a set of pre-written messages that go out on a schedule — without you pressing send. When a new lead comes in, the system handles the first few touches while you focus on the job in front of you.

Here's the sequence we build for most of our trades clients:

1. **Minute 0 — Instant confirmation.** Someone submits your form, they get an email straight away: "Got your message. We'll be in touch within 2 hours." You don't touch anything. It tells the customer you're professional, even if you're up a ladder.

2. **Hour 2 — Personal follow-up.** Your CRM pings you that a lead came in. You call or text them. If you can't get to it, the system sends a second message: "Just checking — did you get our confirmation? Happy to jump on a quick call when it suits you."

3. **Day 2 — Value email.** The system sends a short email with something useful — a checklist, a "what to expect" guide, or a quick tip about their enquiry. This isn't a sales pitch. It's proof you know what you're doing.

4. **Day 5 — Soft check-in.** One more touch: "Still thinking about it? No rush — here's what our last client in your area had to say." Include a short testimonial.

5. **Day 14 — Last call.** A final message: "Wanted to check in one last time. If the timing isn't right, no worries — we're here when you're ready." Then the system stops. No one likes being hounded.

That's five touches over two weeks. All pre-written. All automatic. You only step in when someone replies.

We set this exact sequence up for a plumber in Sandringham. Before automation, he was getting to maybe half his leads. After — every single enquiry got a response in under a minute. His close rate went from 20% to 35% in the first month.

## How to Build This in Zoho CRM

You don't need to be technical. Zoho CRM has a built-in workflow engine that handles the timing and sending. Here's how we set it up for our clients.

### Step 1: Set Up Your Lead Capture Form

Use Zoho Forms to create a simple contact form. Embed it on your website. When someone fills it in, Zoho automatically creates a Lead record in your CRM with their name, email, phone, and message.

### Step 2: Write Your Email Templates

Write your 5 follow-up emails using the sequence above as a starting point. Save them in Zoho CRM under Setup > Templates > Email Templates.

Keep them short — 3-5 sentences each. Write like you're texting a mate, not writing a corporate brochure. Use their first name. Skip words like "leverage" and "solutions."

### Step 3: Build the Workflow Rules

Go to Setup > Automation > Workflow Rules. Create one rule per email:

- **Rule 1:** New Lead created → immediately send confirmation email
- **Rule 2:** New Lead created → wait 2 hours → send notification to you
- **Rule 3:** New Lead created → wait 2 days → send value email
- **Rule 4:** New Lead created → wait 5 days → send check-in
- **Rule 5:** New Lead created → wait 14 days → send last call

### Step 4: Add a Stop Condition

You don't want the system emailing someone you're already talking to. In each workflow rule, add a condition: "Only run if Lead Status is still New." Once you change the status to "Contacted" or "In Conversation," the automated emails stop.

### Step 5: Test It

Submit your own form. Watch the emails come in. Check the timing. Read them on your phone — that's where most people will see them.

The whole setup takes about 45 minutes once you have the email copy written.

## The Dollar Cost of Not Following Up

Let's put a number on it. Say you're a plumber getting 20 new enquiries a month. Your average job is worth $800. Follow up with all 20, you might close 8 — that's $6,400.

But if you only get to half because you were busy or forgot, you close 4. That's $3,200 you left on the table. Over a year, that's **$38,400 in lost revenue** — from leads that already wanted to hire you.

We ran these numbers with an HVAC business in Brighton. They were closing about 30% of their leads manually. After we built their automated sequence, their close rate hit 45%. On their average job value of $1,200, that's an extra $5,400/month they weren't getting before.

> **Businesses that respond to leads within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert them** compared to businesses that wait 30 minutes (Lead Connect). An automated system responds in under 60 seconds, every single time, even at midnight on a Sunday.

Automation doesn't replace the human relationship. It bridges the gap between "they enquired" and "you had time to call them back."

It's significantly cheaper than HubSpot once you need more than the free tier. And Zoho Marketing Automation adds lead scoring so you can see which leads are actually interested, not just which ones opened an email.

If you already have a CRM you're happy with, the principles work the same way. The tool matters less than the system.

## Three Mistakes That Kill Your Follow-Up

**1. Writing emails that sound like a robot.** Your follow-up emails should sound like a person. Use first names. Write short sentences. Don't use words like "leverage" or "solutions." If you wouldn't say it to a customer standing in front of you, don't put it in an email.

**2. Too many emails too fast.** Five emails over two weeks is plenty. We've seen businesses set up 10 emails in 7 days and wonder why their unsubscribe rate is through the roof. Give people space.

**3. No way to stop the sequence.** Always build in a stop condition. If someone replies, books a call, or asks to be removed, the system should stop immediately. Nothing kills trust faster than getting an automated "just checking in" after you've already been on the phone with them.

## Common Questions

**How many follow-up emails should I send?**
Bare Bayside Labs recommends 4-5 emails over two weeks for most trades and service businesses. That's enough to stay top-of-mind without being annoying. After the sequence finishes, move non-responsive leads to a monthly newsletter instead.

**How quickly should I respond to a new lead?**
Instantly with automation, and personally within 2 hours. Businesses that respond within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert the lead. An automated confirmation email buys you time to call back.

**Can I automate follow-ups without a CRM?**
Technically yes — you could use Mailchimp or even Gmail templates. But without a CRM, you can't track which leads responded, which went cold, or where they came from. A CRM makes the whole system measurable.
