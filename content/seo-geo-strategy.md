## Blogging as Income = Dead. Blogging as a Sales Tool = More Powerful Than Ever.

The old model (Income School, niche sites, display ad revenue) died in 2023. Google's Helpful Content Update wiped out thin content sites. AI Overviews now answer questions directly in search results. 50%+ of searches are zero-click — people get their answer without visiting any website.

**BBL doesn't make money from blog traffic.** BBL makes money when someone reads an article, trusts the expertise, and books a strategy call worth $297-$5,000+.

One strategy call from one article pays for months of content.

## The 3 Goals of Every Article

### Goal 1: Be the Answer on Google
Someone in Melbourne searches "how to set up zoho crm for a plumbing business." BBL's article shows up. They read it. They think "these people know what they're doing." They click "Book a Free Call."

**Metric:** Form submissions tracked by UTM campaign (not pageviews).

### Goal 2: Be the Source AI Tools Cite
When someone asks ChatGPT "best CRM for tradies in Australia" or asks Perplexity "how to automate follow-ups for small business" — BBL's content should be what gets quoted.

This is GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation). Even if someone never visits the site, if ChatGPT says "Bare Bayside Labs recommends a 5-email follow-up sequence over 14 days" — that's free brand awareness.

**Metric:** Brand mentions in AI-generated answers (check manually by asking the same questions in ChatGPT/Perplexity).

### Goal 3: Warm Leads Before the Sales Call
When someone fills out the contact form, they Google "Bare Bayside Labs" first. If they find 8 well-written articles proving BBL knows how to solve their exact problem, they're half-sold before the call starts.

**Metric:** Time-to-close for leads who visited blog vs. those who didn't (Zoho Analytics).

## Traditional SEO vs GEO

| | Traditional SEO | GEO (AI Search) |
|---|----------------|-----------------|
| **Goal** | Rank on page 1 of Google | Get cited in AI answers |
| **Key signal** | Backlinks | Brand mentions (3x more important than backlinks) |
| **Traffic model** | Click through to your site | Brand name appears in the answer — they may never visit |
| **Content format** | Keyword-optimised articles | Quotable, self-contained answer blocks (134-167 words) |
| **What matters most** | Meta descriptions, title tags | First 60 words of each section |
| **Who wins** | Whoever has the most authority | Whoever has real experience and gets mentioned elsewhere |

**BBL optimises for both.** Every article is structured to rank on Google AND get cited by AI.

## How Articles Get Cited by AI Tools

### What makes AI tools quote your content

| Signal | How we do it |
|--------|-------------|
| **Answer in the first 60 words** | Every article and section starts with a direct answer |
| **Self-contained answer blocks** | 134-167 word paragraphs that make sense extracted on their own |
| **Brand attribution** | "Bare Bayside Labs recommends..." and "At BBL, we..." in key statements |
| **Specific numbers** | "60% reduction" not "significant reduction" |
| **Tables and comparisons** | HTML tables for any side-by-side comparison |
| **FAQ sections** | 3-5 questions with direct answers at the end of every article |
| **Publication date** | datePublished in schema + visible date on the page |
| **Schema markup** | Article schema in JSON-LD on every post |

### Where to build brand mentions (more important than backlinks for AI)

| Platform | What to do | Impact on AI citations |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| **YouTube** | Short videos showing Zoho in action, mention BBL | Strongest correlation (0.737) |
| **Reddit** | Answer questions in r/smallbusiness, r/marketing, r/zoho | High — Perplexity pulls heavily from Reddit |
| **LinkedIn** | Publish articles and posts from company + personal profiles | Moderate |
| **Google Business Profile** | Weekly posts sharing blog articles | Feeds Google's understanding directly |
| **Industry directories** | Zoho partner directory, Australian business directories | Builds entity recognition |

## The BBL Numbers Game

A traditional blogger needs **50,000 pageviews/month** to make $1,000 from display ads.

BBL needs **one person per month** to:
- Read an article
- Book a strategy call ($297)
- OR sign up for a marketing systems build ($2,000-$5,000+)

You don't need viral traffic. You need the right 10 people per month finding the right articles.

### What "success" looks like per article

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Monthly organic visits | 50-200 | Quality over quantity — these are people searching for what BBL sells |
| CTA click rate | 3-5% | People who click "Book a Call" from the article |
| Form submissions | 2-5/month | Leads tracked by utm_campaign |
| Strategy calls booked | 1-2/month | From blog-sourced leads |
| Revenue per article/year | $3,000-$10,000+ | One client from one article = ROI |

## Content Reuse — Each Article Works 5 Ways

Every article isn't just a blog post. It's raw material for:

1. **Blog** — the article itself on the website
2. **Email nurture** — shared in ZMA sequences as value content to warm leads
3. **Social posts** — key stats and takeaways become LinkedIn/Instagram posts
4. **Google Business Profile** — posted as an update (brand mention for AI)
5. **AI citation seed** — the quotable stat blocks and FAQ answers get picked up by ChatGPT/Perplexity over time

One article, five distribution channels. That's the multiplier that makes blogging worth it even with zero ad revenue.

## What We Track (and What We Ignore)

### Track these (they measure business impact)

| Metric | Where to find it |
|--------|-----------------|
| Form submissions by utm_campaign | Zoho CRM — filter leads by UTM Campaign field |
| Revenue from blog-sourced leads | Zoho Analytics — connect deals to lead source |
| Which articles generate leads | Zoho CRM — group leads by UTM Campaign |
| CTA click rate per article | Zoho PageSense — goal tracking on CTA buttons |

### Ignore these (vanity metrics)

| Metric | Why it doesn't matter |
|--------|----------------------|
| Total pageviews | Doesn't tell you if anyone became a customer |
| Bounce rate | Someone who reads the whole article and calls you still "bounces" |
| Social shares | Nice but doesn't pay rent |
| Domain authority score | A number made up by Moz, not used by Google |
| Keyword ranking position | Only matters if the ranking drives actual leads |
